# Useful Resources

This page collects reputable data sources, legal guides, and media coverage focused on ICE operations, detention, and immigration enforcement outcomes.

## Official Data & Dashboards

- **ICE Data Portal** — Monthly CSVs for book-ins, detained population, removals, and Alternatives to Detention statistics. <https://www.ice.gov/statistics>
- **DHS Yearbook of Immigration Statistics** — Annual reference tables detailing arrests, removals, and court outcomes (see “Enforcement Actions” section). <https://www.dhs.gov/immigration-statistics/yearbook>
- **CBP Southwest Border Encounters** — Monthly encounter counts by sector, family unit category, and nationality. <https://www.cbp.gov/newsroom/stats/southwest-land-border-encounters>
- **EOIR Adjudication Statistics** — Court filings, completions, and relief outcomes updated monthly. <https://www.justice.gov/eoir/workload-and-adjudication-statistics>
- **TRAC Immigration (Syracuse University)** — Independent dashboards on detention, ICE deportations, and immigration courts (download CSVs from each visualization). <https://trac.syr.edu/immigration/>

## Facility & Detainee Information

- **ICE Detention Facility Directory** — Interactive map + CSV with facility addresses, field office contacts, and phone numbers. <https://www.ice.gov/detain/detention-facilities>
- **ICE Detainee Locator** — Search for people in ICE custody (requires A-number or biographical details). <https://locator.ice.gov/odls/#/index>
- **ICE Office of the Principal Legal Advisor (OPLA)** — Field office contacts for legal inquiries. <https://www.ice.gov/contact/opla>

## Know Your Rights & Community Guides

- **ACLU: Know Your Rights – Immigrants & ICE** — What to do if approached by ICE, your right to remain silent, and how to prepare a safety plan. <https://www.aclu.org/know-your-rights/immigrants-rights>
- **Immigrant Legal Resource Center (ILRC)** — Community-facing tip sheets on ICE encounters, raid preparedness, and organizing. <https://www.ilrc.org/community-resources>
- **National Immigration Law Center (NILC)** — Legal fact sheets on enforcement priorities and defending against workplace/home raids. <https://www.nilc.org/issues/immigration-enforcement/>
- **United We Dream Deportation Defense Guide** — Rapid response steps and hotline info for families facing arrests. <https://unitedwedream.org/resources/deportation-defense/>

## Statistics on Prior Convictions

- **ICE FY ERO Annual Report** — Includes tables showing the percentage of arrests/removals involving people with prior criminal convictions vs. civil immigration violations. <https://www.ice.gov/library/reports/ero-annual-report>
- **DHS Enforcement Tables 38–41 (Yearbook)** — Breakdowns of removals by criminality and field office; useful for calculating ratios of “no conviction” removals. <https://www.dhs.gov/immigration-statistics/yearbook/2022>

## Media & Commentary

- **MeidasTouch & Brian Tyler Cohen** — Frequent segments on ICE policy shifts and whistleblower revelations. <https://www.meidastouch.com> | <https://briantylercohen.substack.com/>
- **DocumentedNY** — Local NYC reporting on ICE raids, detention trends, and data-driven investigations. <https://documentedny.com/>
- **The Marshall Project** — Investigative stories on immigration detention conditions and due process. <https://www.themarshallproject.org/>

## Courts & Legal Filings

- **PACER / CourtListener** — Source documents for federal litigation involving ICE practices (search by case number or keyword). <https://www.courtlistener.com/>
- **American Immigration Council – Litigation** — Public docket summaries and filings for major immigration cases. <https://www.americanimmigrationcouncil.org/impact/litigation>

## Using These Resources

- For near-real-time metrics, pull the monthly CSVs from the ICE portal or TRAC downloads.
- For historical comparisons or ratios of criminal vs. non-criminal arrests/removals, rely on DHS Yearbook tables and the annual ICE ERO report.
- When advising communities, share the ACLU/ILRC/NILC guides so people know their rights during ICE encounters.
- Link facility lookups directly to ICE’s official directory to ensure addresses and contact numbers remain up to date.
